 # **Customer Retention & Churn Analysis Dashboard**

An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze customer churn patterns and identify key factors affecting customer retention. This project helps businesses understand why customers leave and provides insights to improve retention strategies.

# Project Overview

Customer churn is a major issue for subscription-based businesses. This project focuses on analyzing customer data and visualizing patterns that lead to churn.
The dashboard provides a data-driven approach to:

- Identify at-risk customers
- Understand churn behavior
- Improve business decision-making
  
# Objectives
Identify customers likely to churn
Analyze key factors influencing churn
Provide actionable insights for retention
Support data-driven strategies

# Tools & Technologies
Power BI
Data Visualization
Business Intelligence
Data Analytics

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Total Customers
Churn Customers
Average Monthly Charges
These KPIs provide a quick snapshot of customer retention performance.

 # Dashboard Features & Analysis
 Analysis and Findings:

1. Monthly Charges vs Churn:
The analysis shows that customers who did not churn contribute higher total monthly charges, while churned customers have lower spending. This indicates that customers with lower engagement and spending are more likely to leave.

2. Churn Distribution (Pie Chart):
The churn distribution shows that the majority of customers are retained, while a smaller portion represents churned customers. This indicates that although retention is strong, churn still exists and needs attention.

3. Contract Type vs Churn:
The analysis reveals that customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate compared to customers with one-year or two-year contracts. Long-term contracts significantly improve customer retention.

4. Tenure Trend:
The trend analysis based on tenure shows that customers who stay longer are less likely to churn. New customers, especially within the initial period, have a higher risk of leaving.

5. Monthly Charges & Tenure vs Churn:
The analysis shows that non-churn customers have higher tenure and higher monthly charges, while churned customers have lower values. This indicates that long-term and high-value customers are more loyal and less likely to churn.
